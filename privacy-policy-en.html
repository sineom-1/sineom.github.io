2. How We Use Cookies and Similar Technologies

2.1 Cookies

To ensure the normal operation of the website, we will store small data files called Cookies on your computer or mobile device. Cookies usually contain identifiers, site names and some numbers and characters. With the help of Cookies, websites can store your preferences and other data.

We use Cookies mainly to achieve the following functions:

  • Identity verification: Cookies can help us confirm your login status and avoid repeated login.
  • Security: Cookies can help us ensure the security of data and services, and check for cheating, hacking, fraud and other behaviors against our products and services.
  • Preference settings: Cookies can help us understand your preferences and habits, helping you save the steps and processes of repeatedly filling in personal information and entering search content.
  • Analysis and research: We use Cookies to learn about improving our products or services.

2.2 Web Beacons and Pixel Tags

In addition to Cookies, we also use other similar technologies such as web beacons on the website. Our web pages often contain some electronic images (called "single pixel" GIF files or "web beacons"). We use web beacons in the following ways:

  • By using web beacons on our website, count user visits and identify registered users by accessing Cookies.
  • Use the obtained Cookie information to provide you with personalized services.

2.3 Your Choices

Most browsers provide users with the function of managing Cookies. When users perform browser Cookie settings, they can allow trusted websites to enable Cookie functions and prohibit other websites from using Cookie functions. You can clear Cookies stored on your computer through browser settings. But if you do this, you may need to manually change user settings every time you visit.
